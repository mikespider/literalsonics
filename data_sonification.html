Data Sonification

Transforming numerical data, shapes, and movements into meaningful, musical experiences.

Exploring Data Through Sound

Data Sonification is the practice of translating information into auditory experiences, enabling us to perceive patterns, trends, and relationships through listening rather than visual inspection. At Literal Sonics, we take numerical datasets, spatial structures, and motion sequences and map them into musical parameters such as pitch, rhythm, amplitude, and timbre.

This approach offers a deeply intuitive understanding of complex systems. Where graphs and charts show numbers visually, sonification brings them to life, allowing the brain to detect subtle variations, cyclical patterns, and anomalies that might otherwise remain hidden. Each dataset becomes a living composition, where information is transformed into textures, motifs, and dynamic soundscapes.

Our work bridges art and science. By applying musical logic to raw data, we create pieces that are both informative and aesthetically compelling, turning data into an experience that can be explored, performed, and felt.

Planetary Distances in Sound

One of our signature compositions translates the average distances between the planets in the solar system into sound frequencies. Each planet’s proportional distance from the Sun is converted into hertz, generating a scale that represents the structure of the solar system in an auditory form.

The result is a celestial symphony: inner planets create higher, more rapid tones while outer planets produce slower, deeper frequencies. This composition allows listeners to experience the harmony of planetary spacing in a novel and immersive way, hearing the relationships between planetary orbits as a flowing musical landscape.
