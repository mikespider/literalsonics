Data Sonification

Transforming numerical data, shapes, and movements into meaningful, musical experiences.

Exploring Data Through Sound

Data Sonification is the practice of translating information into auditory experiences, enabling us to perceive patterns, trends, and relationships through listening rather than visual inspection. At Literal Sonics, we take numerical datasets, spatial structures, and motion sequences and map them into musical parameters such as pitch, rhythm, amplitude, and timbre.

This approach offers a deeply intuitive understanding of complex systems. Where graphs and charts show numbers visually, sonification brings them to life, allowing the brain to detect subtle variations, cyclical patterns, and anomalies that might otherwise remain hidden.

Our work bridges art and science by turning data into compositions that can be explored and felt.

Planetary Distances in Sound

One of our signature compositions translates the average distances between the planets into sound frequencies. Each planet becomes a tone in a harmonic structure representing the solar system.

Inner planets generate higher tones while outer planets produce deeper frequencies, forming a celestial sonic gradient.
